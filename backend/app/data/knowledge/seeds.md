# Seeds and Seed Treatment

## Choosing good seed
- Prefer **certified or truthfully-labelled seed** from a licensed dealer.
  Check the tag for variety, lot number, germination % and validity date.
- Good seed should have **germination above 85%** for most crops.
- Keep the bill — it is needed for any compensation claim if seed fails.

## Seed rate (per acre, typical)
- Wheat: 40-50 kg
- Paddy (transplanted): 8-10 kg nursery seed
- Maize: 8-10 kg
- Gram/chickpea: 25-30 kg
- Mustard: 1.5-2 kg
- Tomato (nursery): 80-100 g
- Onion (nursery): 3-4 kg

## Why treat seed before sowing
Seed treatment protects the young seedling from soil-borne fungi, insects and
seed-borne diseases during the most vulnerable first weeks. It is cheap
insurance and improves the plant stand.

## Seed treatment steps
1. **Fungicide**: treat with Carbendazim or Thiram at 2-3 g per kg of seed to
   prevent damping-off and root rots. For smut diseases in wheat use
   Carboxin/Tebuconazole.
2. **Insecticide** (where soil pests/termites are a problem): Imidacloprid or
   Thiamethoxam seed dressing.
3. **Bio-inoculant**: for pulses apply **Rhizobium** culture; for cereals and
   vegetables apply **Azotobacter/Azospirillum** and **PSB**. Always add the
   bio-culture **last**, after the chemical treatment has dried.
4. Dry treated seed in shade and sow the **same day**.

Order to remember: **Fungicide → Insecticide → Bio-inoculant**.

## Germination test at home
Count 100 seeds, place between moist cloth/newspaper, keep warm for 5-7 days,
then count how many sprout. If fewer than 80-85 sprout, increase the seed rate
or buy fresh seed.

## Hybrid vs open-pollinated
- **Hybrid (F1) seed** gives higher, more uniform yield but must be bought
  fresh every season — saved seed will not breed true.
- **Open-pollinated/desi varieties** can be saved and resown, are cheaper and
  often hardier, but yield less.

## Seed storage
Store dry seed (below 10-12% moisture) in airtight containers with a little
neem leaf or a dryer sachet, in a cool dry place away from rodents.
