# Fertilizer Guide for Indian Farmers

## Understanding NPK
Fertilizers are graded by three numbers, e.g. 19:19:19. These are the
percentages of Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P) and Potassium (K).
- **Nitrogen (N)** drives leafy, green vegetative growth. Deficiency shows as
  pale yellow older leaves and stunted plants.
- **Phosphorus (P)** builds strong roots, flowering and fruiting. Deficiency
  shows as purplish leaves and poor root growth.
- **Potassium (K)** improves fruit quality, disease resistance and water use.
  Deficiency shows as scorched leaf margins.

## Common straight fertilizers
- **Urea (46% N)**: cheapest source of nitrogen. Apply as top dressing in
  splits. Do not apply on waterlogged soil. Typical: 100-130 kg/acre for wheat,
  split across sowing and irrigations.
- **DAP (18% N, 46% P)**: a basal fertilizer applied at sowing for root and
  early growth. Typical: 40-50 kg/acre at sowing.
- **MOP / Muriate of Potash (60% K)**: potassium source for fruiting crops.
- **SSP / Single Super Phosphate (16% P + sulphur + calcium)**: good for
  oilseeds, pulses and where sulphur is needed.

## Complex and water-soluble fertilizers
- **19:19:19 (all-purpose NPK)**: balanced, ideal for the vegetative stage and
  for foliar spray. A good general-purpose fertilizer for most vegetables.
- **12:61:00 (MAP)**: high phosphorus, used at flowering/early fruit set.
- **00:00:50 (SOP) or 13:00:45**: high potassium, used during fruit development
  to improve size, colour and shelf life.
- **Calcium nitrate**: corrects calcium deficiency (e.g. blossom-end rot in
  tomato and capsicum).

## Organic and bio-fertilizers
- **Farmyard manure (FYM / gobar khaad)**: 2-3 tonnes/acre mixed before sowing.
  Improves soil structure and water holding.
- **Vermicompost**: nutrient-rich, 400-800 kg/acre.
- **Neem cake**: adds nitrogen and suppresses soil pests and nematodes.
- **Bio-fertilizers**: Rhizobium (pulses), Azotobacter/Azospirillum (cereals,
  vegetables) and PSB (phosphate solubilising bacteria) fix or release
  nutrients naturally. Treat seed or soil before sowing.
- **Jeevamrut / green manure (dhaincha, sunhemp)**: cheap ways to build soil
  organic carbon.

## Micronutrients
Zinc, boron, iron and sulphur deficiencies are common in Indian soils.
- **Zinc sulphate**: 10 kg/acre in zinc-deficient soils (common in rice-wheat
  belt). Corrects khaira disease in rice and white-bud in maize.
- **Boron (borax)**: important for flowering and fruit set; deficiency causes
  hollow stem and cracked fruit.
- **Ferrous sulphate**: corrects iron chlorosis (yellowing between veins of
  young leaves), often via foliar spray.

## How to decide the right dose
1. Always start from a **soil test** — it tells you what is actually lacking.
2. Apply phosphorus and potassium fully as **basal** at sowing.
3. Apply nitrogen in **splits** (basal + at each irrigation/critical stage).
4. Do not over-apply urea: excess nitrogen causes lush weak growth, more pests
   and lodging.
5. Mix organic manure every season to keep soil alive.

## All-purpose fertilizer for a mixed farm
For a smallholder growing several vegetables and flowers, a practical kit is:
- Basal: FYM + DAP (or SSP) + a little MOP.
- Vegetative stage: 19:19:19 foliar/soil.
- Flowering: 12:61:00.
- Fruiting: 00:00:50 or 13:00:45.
- Plus zinc and boron once per season if soil test shows deficiency.
