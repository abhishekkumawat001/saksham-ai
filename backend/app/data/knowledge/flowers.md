# Flower (Floriculture) Cultivation

## Marigold (genda)
- **Season**: can be grown almost year round; major crops for festivals
  (Dussehra, Diwali). Nursery 25-30 days, transplant at 30-45 cm spacing.
- **Pinching**: remove the growing tip ~30-40 days after transplanting to
  encourage more branches and flowers.
- **Fertilizer**: FYM basal; balanced NPK; extra phosphorus and potash at
  budding for more and bigger blooms.
- **Pests**: leaf hopper, red spider mite; Alternaria leaf spot in humid
  weather.

## Rose
- **Propagation**: by budding/grafting on rootstock or cuttings.
- **Pruning**: annual hard pruning in October (north India) triggers strong
  flowering flush.
- **Fertilizer**: heavy feeder — FYM plus NPK monthly; potash improves bloom
  colour and stem strength.
- **Diseases**: black spot, powdery mildew and dieback; spray suitable
  fungicide and keep good air circulation.

## Chrysanthemum (guldaudi)
- **Short-day plant**: flowers when days get shorter. Pinching gives bushy
  plants. Stake tall varieties.

## Gerbera and Carnation (polyhouse crops)
- Grown in **protected cultivation (polyhouse)** on raised beds with drip
  fertigation for high-value cut-flower production.
- Maintain good drainage; root rot (Phytophthora/Pythium) is the main risk.

## Gladiolus
- Grown from **corms**. Plant in rows, earth-up for support, and lift corms
  after the foliage dries for storage and replanting.

## Jasmine (mogra/chameli)
- Perennial shrub; prune in winter; flowers in summer. Good for loose-flower
  (gajra) and oil markets.

## General floriculture tips
- Flowers need **good drainage** — waterlogging quickly causes root rot.
- Harvest at the correct bud stage (tight bud or just opening, depending on the
  flower and market) in the cool morning hours.
- For cut flowers, place stems in clean water immediately and keep cool.
- Marigold and other flowers are also used as **border/trap crops** in
  vegetable fields to attract pests away and host beneficial insects.
