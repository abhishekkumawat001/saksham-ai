# Vegetable Cultivation Guide

## Tomato
- **Season**: rabi (Oct-Nov nursery) and kharif; protected/summer crops too.
- **Nursery to transplant**: raise seedlings 25-30 days, transplant at 60x45 cm.
- **Fertilizer**: FYM basal; 19:19:19 in vegetative stage; 12:61:00 at
  flowering; potash (00:00:50) at fruiting. Add calcium nitrate to prevent
  blossom-end rot.
- **Key pests/diseases**: fruit borer (spray as per label), whitefly (vector of
  leaf curl virus — control with neem oil/imidacloprid), early and late blight
  (mancozeb), damping-off in nursery (seed treatment + good drainage).
- **Yellowing leaves**: usually nitrogen deficiency or waterlogging; if leaves
  also curl, suspect leaf curl virus and control whitefly.

## Onion
- **Season**: rabi nursery Oct-Nov, transplant Dec-Jan; kharif sowing Jun-Jul.
- **Soil**: well-drained loam, pH 6-7.
- **Fertilizer**: nitrogen in splits, plenty of phosphorus and potash, plus
  sulphur for pungency and storage.
- **Key issues**: thrips (silvery streaks on leaves), purple blotch
  (mancozeb), bolting if sown too early.

## Chilli
- **Season**: kharif and summer.
- **Fertilizer**: balanced NPK with extra potash for fruiting.
- **Key pests**: thrips and whitefly cause leaf curl ("murda/leaf curl
  complex"); spray fipronil or imidacloprid and use neem oil; control mites
  with appropriate acaricide.

## Potato
- **Season**: rabi, planting Oct-Nov in the plains.
- **Fertilizer**: high requirement of N, P and K; earthing-up needed.
- **Key disease**: late blight (Phytophthora) in cool humid weather — spray
  mancozeb preventively, cymoxanil+mancozeb in severe attack.

## Brinjal (eggplant)
- **Key pest**: shoot and fruit borer — remove and destroy bored shoots, use
  pheromone traps, spray as per label.

## Okra (bhindi)
- **Season**: summer and kharif. Watch for yellow vein mosaic virus (whitefly
  vector) — grow resistant varieties.

## Cucurbits (gourds, cucumber, pumpkin)
- **Key pest**: fruit fly — use pheromone/cue-lure traps and bait splashes.
- Downy and powdery mildew controlled with appropriate fungicides.

## General vegetable tips
- Rotate crops; do not grow the same family (e.g. tomato-potato-brinjal, all
  Solanaceae) in the same plot every season — it builds up soil disease.
- Mulching conserves moisture and suppresses weeds.
- Drip irrigation plus fertigation saves water and improves yield.
