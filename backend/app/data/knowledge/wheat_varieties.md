# Wheat Cultivation and Varieties

## Wheat growing season
Wheat is a **rabi (winter) crop** in India. Ideal sowing is **late October to
late November** in the north. Each week of delay after mid-November can lower
yield, because grain filling then meets the late-season heat.

## Popular high-yielding varieties
- **HD 2967**: widely grown, lodging tolerant, good rust resistance, suited to
  north-western and north-eastern plains. Maturity ~150 days.
- **HD 3086 (Pusa Gautami)**: high yield, good rust resistance, timely sown
  irrigated conditions.
- **HD 3226 (Pusa Yashasvi)**: high protein, resistant to multiple rusts and
  some leaf diseases.
- **DBW 187 (Karan Vandana)**: early maturing, high yield, suited to early
  sowing in eastern plains.
- **DBW 222 / DBW 303**: high-yielding recent releases for irrigated timely
  sowing.
- **PBW 343 / PBW 550**: older but still grown in Punjab region.
- **WH 1105**: north-western plains, lodging and rust tolerant.
- **Durum wheat (e.g. HI 8759 Pusa Tejas)**: hard wheat for semolina/suji and
  pasta, grown in central India.

Always pick a variety **notified for your zone** and matched to your sowing
time (early/timely/late) and irrigation availability.

## Seed and sowing
- Seed rate: 40-50 kg/acre for timely sowing; increase to 50-60 kg for late
  sowing.
- Treat seed against loose smut and termites before sowing.
- Sow at 4-5 cm depth with row spacing of about 20-22 cm using a seed drill for
  even stand.

## Fertilizer for wheat (per acre, adjust by soil test)
- Nitrogen: 50-60 kg (≈110-130 kg urea), split: at sowing, first irrigation
  (21-25 days, crown root stage) and second irrigation.
- Phosphorus: ~25 kg P2O5 (DAP/SSP) as basal.
- Potassium: ~12 kg K2O where soil is deficient.
- Apply zinc sulphate if the field is in a zinc-deficient rice-wheat belt.

## Irrigation — critical stages
The most yield-sensitive stages are:
1. Crown root initiation (20-25 days) — the most critical single irrigation.
2. Tillering.
3. Jointing.
4. Flowering.
5. Milk/dough (grain filling).
A typical irrigated crop needs 4-6 irrigations.

## Common problems
- **Yellow / brown rust**: orange-brown pustules on leaves. Grow resistant
  varieties; spray Propiconazole if it appears.
- **Loose smut**: black powdery heads; controlled by seed treatment.
- **Lodging** (crop falling over): avoid excess nitrogen and choose stiff-straw
  varieties.
- **Karnal bunt**: managed by resistant varieties and seed treatment.

## Harvest
Harvest at physiological maturity when grain is hard and moisture is ~ 12-14%.
