# General Farming, Soil and Irrigation

## Soil health
- Get a **soil test** every 2-3 years for pH, organic carbon and N-P-K plus
  micronutrients. Apply lime in acidic soils and gypsum in sodic/alkaline soils.
- Keep **organic carbon** up with FYM, compost, green manure and crop residue
  incorporation. Healthy soil holds water and nutrients better.
- Practise **crop rotation** (e.g. cereal → legume) to break pest cycles and fix
  nitrogen.

## Irrigation methods
- **Flood/furrow**: simple but wastes water.
- **Drip irrigation**: delivers water to the root zone, saves 40-60% water,
  ideal for vegetables, flowers, orchards; pairs with fertigation.
- **Sprinkler**: good for close-spaced crops and undulating land.
- Water at **critical growth stages** rather than fixed dates; avoid both
  drought stress and waterlogging.

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
1. Use resistant varieties and clean, treated seed.
2. Encourage natural enemies; use pheromone and sticky traps for monitoring.
3. Use **neem-based** and biological pesticides first.
4. Spray chemical pesticides only at the economic threshold, rotating modes of
   action to avoid resistance. Always read the label and observe the
   pre-harvest interval. Wear gloves and a mask.

## Weed management
- Keep the first 30-45 days weed-free for most crops.
- Combine manual/mechanical weeding, mulching and recommended herbicides.

## Spraying basics
- For most pesticides mix **30-40 ml or g in a 15-litre knapsack pump**, but
  always follow the label — products differ widely.
- Spray in the cool morning or evening, not in strong sun or wind.

## Government support
- **Soil Health Card** scheme provides free soil testing recommendations.
- **PM-KISAN** gives income support to farmer families.
- **Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)** is the crop insurance scheme.
- **Kisan Credit Card (KCC)** provides low-interest crop loans.
Contact your local Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or agriculture officer for advice.

## When to consult an expert
If a problem spreads quickly, affects a large area, or you are unsure of the
pest/disease, take a sample or photo to the nearest KVK or input dealer before
spraying randomly. Wrong chemicals waste money and can harm the crop.
